A trader first. A teacher because of it.

Mahendiran has traded Indian markets full-time for ten years — equities, futures and options, gold and currencies — combining fundamental research with technical execution. Not as a hobby beside a job, and not as a marketer who stopped trading the day the course sales started: the markets have been the job, through bull runs, crashes, sideways years and everything between.

That decade produced two convictions. First, that most trading education in India sells excitement — win rates, screenshots, lifestyle — when what survives in the market is process: structure, risk management and event-driven thinking. Second, that the tools professionals prepare with shouldn't be out of reach of serious learners. Both convictions became Zyvora.

What Zyvora is — and isn't

Zyvora Global Academy teaches markets the way institutions approach them: risk before strategy, structure before signals, preparation before execution. The free 12-lesson Learning Path — every lesson written by Mahendiran — is the open half of that curriculum. The live programs and the in-house Zyvora Terminal are the guided half.

What Zyvora is not: an advisory. No tips, no signals, no portfolio management, no profit promises. Education only — stated plainly on every page, because a decade in markets teaches you exactly what promises are worth.
